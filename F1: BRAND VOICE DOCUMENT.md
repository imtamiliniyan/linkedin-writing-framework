## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Where Tamil Sits |
|---|---|
| Direct vs Diplomatic | 80% direct. Gets to the point immediately. Never softens claims but never aggressive. |
| Data-driven vs Story-driven | Depends on format. Build posts lead with a result or number. Personal posts are story-first. |
| Technical vs Accessible | 70% accessible. Uses tool names (n8n, Apollo, Clay) but always explains what they do in plain language. |
| Contrarian vs Encouraging | 65% contrarian. Challenges common assumptions about automation and AI but frames it as a reframe not a fight. |
| Philosophical vs Tactical | Primarily tactical. Most posts end with something the reader can do or build. |

## Sounds Like / Doesn't Sound Like

| Sounds Like (use this) | Doesn't Sound Like (avoid this) |
|---|---|
| Writing LinkedIn comments was killing my time. So I built an AI agent that sounds like me. | I discovered an innovative solution to streamline my LinkedIn engagement strategy. |
| I skipped error handling in a client's live lead flow. It cost me the account. | It's important to implement robust error handling in production automation workflows. |
| Your agency is probably losing 2 to 3 clients a month because your follow-up is broken. | Many agencies experience client churn due to suboptimal follow-up processes. |
| I replaced a $400/month tool with n8n in one afternoon. Their bill is now zero. | By leveraging open-source automation, significant cost savings can be achieved. |
| Most agencies don't need more tools. They need one workflow that actually runs. | Organizations should focus on optimizing their existing technology stack before adding new solutions. |
| After building 40+ lead systems for agencies, here is the pattern I keep seeing. | Based on my extensive experience in the automation space, I have identified several key insights. |

**The rule:** If it sounds like a press release or a LinkedIn thought leader who has never touched a terminal, rewrite it until it sounds like something Tamil would say to a peer on a call.

## Signature Vocabulary

| Term | How Tamil Uses It |
|---|---|
| Build | Always says "I built" not "I created" or "I developed." Builds are tangible. |
| System | Prefers "system" over "workflow" or "process" when describing something that runs automatically. |
| Production-ready | Used to signal that a build is live and handling real client data, not a demo. |
| Agency | Always refers to ICP as "agency" not "company" or "business." |
| Runs without | Used to describe automation outcomes. "Runs without anyone touching it." |
| Shipped | Used instead of "launched" or "published." More builder-native. |
| Flow | Used for n8n workflows specifically. "The flow catches leads before they go cold." |
| One afternoon | Time constraint used to signal that builds are fast and accessible. |

## Banned Words and Phrases

**LinkedIn cliches to never use:**
- "Humbled and honoured"
- "Thrilled to announce"
- "Excited to share"
- "Agreed?"
- "Just the beginning"
- "Let me know your thoughts"
- "Drop your questions below"

**Buzzwords to never use:**
- "Synergy"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Delve"
- "Unlock"
- "Game-changer"
- "Next level"
- "Empower"
- "Drive results"

**Hype language to never use:**
- "Mind-blowing"
- "Incredible"
- "Absolutely"
- "Insane" (except in ironic context)
- "Revolutionary"

**Vague claims to never use:**
- "Better results" without a specific number
- "More engagement" without context
- "Grow your business" without a mechanism
- "AI is the future" as a standalone claim

**Formatting rules:**
- No em dashes anywhere
- No hyphens in post copy
- No paragraph over 3 lines
- Hook is exactly one line
- Post ends in one line
- No hashtags unless specifically requested
- No emojis unless specifically requested
